About J.P.

Who Is J.P.?

J.P. falls into the category of the world's most unique and radical individuals.

One of God's own prototypes. A high-powered mutant of some kind never even considered for mass production. Too weird to live, and too rare to die.
- Hunter S. Thompson

Hailing from a small town in Texas, J.P. started rock climbing and wakeboarding at a very young age. He also took up snowboarding. It was clear that he enjoyed taking moderate risks and focusing on nothing in life except having as much fun as possible, all day e'rrrry day. He has had a thirst for life since birth.

Fast-forward to his '20s, and J.P. decided it was time to up the game, so he signed up for what's known as 'Accelerated freefall', or AFF for short. This is the program where you learn how to skydive. After about 8 jumps with instructors by your side, they turn you loose into the sky on your own and let you do your solo jumps until you reach jump number 25, at which point you go up and do a final check dive with an instructor. You do a series of maneuvers and if you perform all of them, in a safe manner, you earn yourself a USPA 'A' license, which means you are a big bad skydiver and can jump with your own gear, with other people, at any dropzone in the world.

Next, things got real. J.P. started BASE jumping full on stoke mode, with zero holding back. Over the course of a few years J.P. racked up over 300 BASE jumps, including all the categories of BASE (buildings, antenna towers, bridges (spans), earth). In the videos section below, you can see J.P. nonchalantly break his shinbone on a perfectly normal jump off the Perrine Bridge in Twin Falls, ID. Without needing to say, a little broken bone has not stopped him from sending it full time.

When not throwing himself out of airplanes, or off the beautiful cliffs of Moab, UT, or discovering unopened exit points from gorgeous cliffs in the Rocky Mountains, J.P. has been a bit of a slacklining protege for the last 2 years, learning how to rig slacklines and to build funky space nets with all kinds of rock climbing rope and hardware.
